System Requirements Document Project:  Front End Payment Management System for Village Health Workers (VHWs)  Client:  Ministry of Health and Child Care (MoHCC)  Purpose:  To strengthen internal controls, eliminate payment duplications, and prepare for secure  EcoCash  system integration while maintaining decentralised operations. 1. Background and Context The Ministry of Health and Child Care currently manages quarterly payments for approximately 20,000 Village Health Workers (VHWs) using a decentralised Excel-based database. While functional, the manual process exposes the Ministry to risks such as: Post-verification alterations to payment lists Payments to exited or inactive beneficiaries Weak segregation of duties Duplicate or overlapping payments across cycles Heavy reliance on manual validation To address these risks, MoHCC requires a  front-end payment management system  that enforces controls, manages workflows, and integrates seamlessly with  Econet’s   EcoCash  platform. 2. Objectives of the System The system shall: Eliminate duplication errors in the payment cycle. Enforce segregation of duties across Finance, HR, and Provincial levels. Prevent payments to ineligible or exited beneficiaries. Provide audit-ready records and reports. Maintain decentralised provincial operations with central oversight. Enable secure integration with  EcoCash  APIs for automated payments. 3. Scope of the System In Scope Web-based front-end application Role-based access control Payment workflow management Duplicate detection and prevention mechanisms Reporting and reconciliation Integration readiness with  EcoCash  APIs Out of Scope (Phase 1) Direct  EcoCash  API integration (to follow after readiness testing) Replacement of the existing database 4. Users and Roles (Role-Based Access Control) Role Responsibilities Restrictions Provincial Officers Initiate payment lists, verify eligibility, submit evidence (attendance registers, supervisor sign-off). Cannot edit beneficiary details after submission. HR / Programme Custodians Maintain master data, add new VHWs, update status (Active/Inactive/Exited). Cannot initiate payments. Finance Officers Initiate payment batches, validate against certified lists, monitor payment status. Cannot edit beneficiary records. National Oversight / Administrators Read-only access, unlock records with justification, monitor audit logs. Cannot bypass audit trail. 5. Functional Requirements 5.1 User Authentication and Access Secure login with role-based access Session timeouts and password policies 5.2 Beneficiary Management Display beneficiary details (Name, National ID,  EcoCash  number, Province, District, Village, Status). Only  Active  beneficiaries eligible for payment. Automatic flagging of duplicates (same ID or  EcoCash  number). 5.3 Payment Cycle Management Unique cycle ID per quarter (e.g., Q2 2026). Beneficiary can only be paid once per cycle. Duplicate detection across provinces/districts. 5.4 Verification and Certification Workflow Provincial officers submit verified lists with supporting evidence. Head Office certifies lists. System locks records post-certification. Exceptions require administrator justification and audit logging. 5.5 Payment Initiation Finance initiates payment batches by province/district. System validates: Beneficiary status Duplicate payments Mandatory data completeness Payment instructions prepared for  EcoCash  integration. 5.6 Reporting and Reconciliation Reports on paid, failed, and excluded beneficiaries. Payment summaries by province, district, and national totals. Reconciliation between certified lists and executed batches. Exportable in PDF/Excel. 5.7 Audit Trail Logs all user actions (add, edit, verify, certify, pay). Captures timestamps, old/new values, and override reasons. Tamper-proof audit records. 6. Non-Functional Requirements Security:  Encrypted credentials, secure storage, role-based access. Reliability:  Accessible during business hours, regular backups. Usability:  Simple, intuitive interface designed for Finance, HR, and Provincial workflows. 7. Integration Readiness (Future Phase) API-ready architecture to send payment instructions to  EcoCash . Receive payment confirmations and error codes (e.g., failed transactions, duplicates). Integration tested in UAT before production rollout. 8. Assumptions and Dependencies Existing database remains authoritative. Clean, approved data provided to ICT. User requirements signed off before development. EcoCash  integration dependent on readiness and NDA agreements. 9. Success Criteria The system will be considered successful if it: Eliminates duplicate payments across cycles. Prevents payments to exited beneficiaries. Enforces segregation of duties. Produces audit-ready reports. Is accepted and actively used by MoHCC and Econet stakeholders.
